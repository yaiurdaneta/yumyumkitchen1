Yum Yum Kitchen Fresh food done right

Ingredients

  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter (1/2 stick)
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1 1/2 cups (12 ounces) Belgian beer
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 3/4 cups flour

Belgian Crepes cooking instructions

  1. Melt 2 tablespoons of the butter in a small saucepan over medium heat. Transfer the melted butter to a large bowl and add the milk and beer, whisking to combine. Whisk in the eggs one at a time, making sure each is incorporated into the milk mixture before adding the next egg. Add the salt and flour, and whisk until the batter is smooth.
  2. Cover the bowl with a towel and allow the batter to rest at room temperature for 1 hour
  3. When you are ready to cook, melt the remaining 2 tablespoons of butter in a small saucepan over medium heat and remove the pan from the heat.
  4. Heat a 10-inch nonstick skillet or crepe pan over medium heat. When it is hot, brush it with a thin coating of the melted butter and pour in a scant 1/3 cup of batter. Lift the skillet from the heat and swirl it to distribute the batter evenly in as thin a layer as possible. Pour any excess batter back into the bowl.
  5. Return the skillet to the heat and cook the crepe for 2 minutes. Give the pan a shake to dislodge the crepe, then use a large spatula or turner to flip the crepe, and cook it for 30 seconds.
  6. Transfer the crepe to a plate, and cover the plate with a tent of aluminum foil. Repeat the process until all of the batter has been used.
  7. Serve the crepes with your choice of fillings and/or toppings.
